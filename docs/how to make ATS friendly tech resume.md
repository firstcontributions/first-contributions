# How to Make an ATS-Friendly Tech Resume

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by companies to manage recruitment. It scans and ranks resumes based on keywords, formatting, and other criteria. To ensure your tech resume gets past the ATS, follow these guidelines:

## Key Principles for ATS Friendliness:

1.  **Use Standard Headings:** Stick to common headings like "Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Projects," and "About Me" (or "Summary"). Avoid creative or unusual heading names that an ATS might not recognize.
2.  **Keyword Optimization:**
    *   Carefully read the job description and identify key skills, tools, and technologies.
    *   Integrate these keywords naturally throughout your resume, especially in your "Skills" and "Experience" sections.
    *   Don't just list keywords; demonstrate how you used them in your projects and roles.
3.  **Simple Formatting:**
    *   Avoid complex layouts, graphics, tables, text boxes, headers, and footers. These can confuse an ATS.
    *   Use a clean, simple, and consistent font (e.g., Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) in a readable size (10-12pt for body, 14-16pt for headings).
    *   Maintain consistent spacing and bullet points.
    *   If using templates, choose ones designed for ATS compatibility.
4.  **Chronological Order:** List your experience and education in reverse chronological order (most recent first).
5.  **Quantify Achievements:** Whenever possible, use numbers and data to describe your accomplishments. For example, instead of "Improved website performance," write "Improved website performance by 20% by optimizing database queries."
6.  **Proofread Meticulously:** Typos and grammatical errors can be flagged by an ATS or, worse, by a human recruiter.
7.  **Save as PDF (Usually):** While some ATS systems prefer .docx, PDF is generally a safe bet as it preserves formatting across different systems. Always check the job application instructions; if they specify a format, use that.

## Sections to Include:

*   **Contact Information:** Name, Phone Number, Email, LinkedIn Profile URL, GitHub Profile URL (if applicable).
*   **Summary/Objective (Optional):** A brief paragraph highlighting your key skills and career goals.
*   **Skills:** A comprehensive list of your technical skills, programming languages, frameworks, tools, and methodologies. Categorize them for clarity (e.g., "Programming Languages:", "Frameworks:", "Tools:").
*   **Experience:** Detail your work history, including company name, location, job title, and dates of employment. Use bullet points to describe your responsibilities and achievements, incorporating keywords and quantifiable results.
*   **Projects:** Showcase personal or open-source projects relevant to the roles you're applying for. Include project name, technologies used, and a brief description of your contribution and impact.
*   **Education:** List your degrees, certifications, institution names, and dates of graduation.
*   **Awards/Certifications (Optional):** Any relevant accolades or professional certifications.

By adhering to these principles, you can significantly increase the chances of your tech resume being successfully parsed by an ATS and reviewed by a human recruiter.
